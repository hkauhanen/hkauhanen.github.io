Useful R functions

crumb / 29 November 2018
#r

\(1 + \int\)

This is a catalogue of R functions that are particularly useful in the sort of work I tend to use R for – analysis of large, multidimensional datasets, including various kinds of Monte Carlo procedures, plus prototyping of computational simulations (and sometimes, when speed is not a major concern, actual simulations). Since I tend to either forget about the existence of these functions or how they are used, however, I thought it might be a good idea to collect them in one place (and this is a dynamic catalogue, meaning that I will be adding more useful functions as I stumble upon them). I hope you will find it useful, too!

dcast

expand.grid

Produces a dataframe of all the possible permutations of values of given variables. This is an extremely useful function in many applications – think about conducting a systematic sweep over the parameter space of a model, for example. You can then loop through the rows of the dataframe and store your dependent variable in another column.

expand.grid(variable1=1:2, variable2=c("a", "b"), variable3=c(TRUE, FALSE))
